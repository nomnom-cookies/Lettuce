About the California Seasonal Produce Guide

This guide is designed to help you discover the freshest, most seasonally available produce grown right here in California. By choosing seasonal produce, you support local farmers, enjoy better flavor and nutrition, and make environmentally conscious choices.

Why Choose Seasonal Produce?

  • Better Flavor: Produce harvested at peak ripeness tastes better and is more flavorful.
  • More Nutrition: Seasonal produce is richer in nutrients and contains higher levels of vitamins and minerals.
  • Lower Cost: When produce is in season, supply is abundant, which means lower prices.
  • Environmental Benefits: Less transportation and storage means a smaller carbon footprint.
  • Support Local: You directly support California farmers and local agriculture.

California's Agricultural Regions

San Francisco Bay Area: Known for diverse crops including citrus, stone fruits, and leafy greens.

Los Angeles Area: Produces avocados, citrus, and subtropical fruits.

San Diego Area: Specializes in avocados, berries, and various vegetables.

Central Valley: The heart of California agriculture, producing vast quantities of grains, vegetables, and nuts.

Inland Empire: Home to citrus, stone fruits, and various vegetables.

Storage Tips

Each produce item in this guide includes specific storage recommendations to help you keep your fresh produce as fresh as possible. Check individual items for storage instructions tailored to that specific fruit or vegetable.
