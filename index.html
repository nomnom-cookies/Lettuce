About the California Seasonal Produce Guide

This guide helps you choose in-season produce, finding the freshest, ripest options that you can consume. By choosing seasonal and local produce, you support your local farmers, enjoy better health and nutrition, and demonstrate your environmental awareness.

Why Choose Seasonal Produce?

  • Better Flavor and Nutrition: All produce grown in their natural season peaks in nutrient content and flavor
  • More Cost-Effective:In-season produce is more abundant and therefore cheaper
  • Benefits the Environment: Seasonal produce, often local, saves energy for transportation and reduces the need for chemical preservatives
  • Supports Local Economy:Supports local farmers and strengthens the community by introducing agricultural diversity

California's Agricultural Regions

San Francisco Bay Area: Known for a diverse range of produce, such as kale, carrots, beets, and artichokes.

Los Angeles Area: Known for a wide variety of year-round produce, such as avocados, citrus, and subtropical fruits.

San Diego Area: Known for its nurseries and floriculture; specializes in avocados, berries, and various vegetables.

Central Valley: Known as the “food basket of America” and the heart of California agriculture, producing vast quantities of grains, vegetables, and nuts.

Inland Empire:Known as the “Inland Empire,” home to citrus, stone fruits, and various vegetables.

Storage Tips

Each produce item listed has its own storage information to keep your fresh produce as fresh as possible. Be sure to check individual item storage information specific to its own fruit/vegetable.
